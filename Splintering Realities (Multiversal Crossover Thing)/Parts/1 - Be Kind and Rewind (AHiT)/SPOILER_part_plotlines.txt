Arc 1 (BKR) Timeline and Events
Crossovers
Source guide

This text outlines the initial narrative arc of a fan-created crossover story, "Be Kind and Rewind," detailing the chaotic consequences when a player named Alex is pulled from the "real world" into the final events of the game A Hat in Time. A pivotal moment is the defeat of Mustache Girl and the subsequent time rewind, which fails to return Alex to her own reality, leaving her out of sync with reality and possessing an anomalous respawn ability. This instability draws the attention of the local characters, leading to a coalition of familiar faces attempting to exploit Alex's unique state, while Alex navigates this new world and eventually finds allies in Hat Kid and Mustache Girl. The arc culminates in Alex being manipulated by the enigmatic Badge Seller (revealed to be the malicious Moonjumper) to engage a device in the inter-universal void, triggering a multiversal collapse that sends the protagonists' spaceship into another universe, setting the stage for further multiversal adventures.
Arc 1 ('Be Kind and Rewind', ft. A Hat in Time):
- Starts with the finale of A Hat in Time
- As Hat Kid goes through Mustache Girl's castle, Alex, a player from the 'real world' playing through A Hat in Time is detected by Mustache Girl, and is pulled through the monitor into the 'game world'
- Mustache Girl has been feeling the 'presence' of Alex during her adventure, particularly around Hat Kid and her spaceship, and revealing her turns her anger toward the intruder
- Alex is knocked out in the encounter
- Then Hat Kid arrives to fight Mu
- Moustache Girl is defeated, and with the power of the Time Pieces the timeline is rewound
- The rewind causes Alex to be out of sync with reality: previously fully part of A Hat in Time's world, when the universe tries to rewind her back to get own universe, it fails because any time travel is impossible in her reality. As a result she is stuck in a limbo state where she is influenced by her realities rules instead of the rules of the world she is in, but respawns instead of truly dying because A Hat in Time's universe can't reconcile the differences properly.
- As a result of the time rewind, events in the adventure of Hat Kid before the story begins change as well - notably, she never loses the Time Pieces in the first place and visits the planet of her own free will.
- Neither Hat Kid, nor any other main character in the story, are aware of the effects of these differences until later, after the rewind
- Finally, those who were present at Hat Kid's final confrontation with Moustache Girl in the time pocket have full recollection of the old timeline, their consciousness jumping from that battle to the new timeline. Alex is also aware. Everyone else has no memory of the old timeline
- Snatcher regains awareness in his home in Subcon Forest, quickly realising that the state of the world has been restored to normal
- Simultaneously, DJ Grooves and the Conductor gain awareness after their sacrifice in the final confrontation, and begin to process that
- Hat Kid regains awareness back in her ship, and is waylaid by Snatcher who is putting the pieces together
- Hat Kid announces her intent to leave the planet and continue her travel home, now that things have been restored
- Alex wakes up, in the spot where Hat Kid first lands in Mafia Town, and tries to figure out what's going on
- She tries to find a place to eat at and is denied, then later drowned - discovering the respawn anomaly
- Snatcher approaches The Conductor, DJ Grooves and the Mafia Boss, seeking to establish a coalition and petition Hat Kid to stay, on the grounds that they respect her power
- Word gets out to the general Mafia population about this plan, and Cooking Cat hears about it, deciding to petition Snatcher to join his coalition, offering what she knows about Hat Kid as leverage
- Hat Kid decides to depart earlier than expected, leading Snatcher to collect his team hastily to intercept and appeal to her
- While picking up the last member, Cooking Cat, the coalition discovers Alex at a cafe, and links her to her presence at Mustache Girl's castle, believing she is part of some unknown plot by Mu
- The coalition fails to appeal to Hat Kid to stay, and as a consequence decides to inform her about Alex, hoping that a task like dealing with this walking anomaly will convince her to stay for a bit longer - this works
- Hat Kid finds Mu still unconscious after their fight, and moves her to Mu's beachside cave
- Mu awakens, and she and HK reconcile about the usage of time pieces
- On the topic of Alex, Mu shares what she knows
- Mu and HK resolve to work together, and help Mu to escape the planet
- independently, Snatcher informs the Subcon inhabitants about Hat Kid's new mission, about Alex, and expresses a desire to capture Alex in the same way he did Hat Kid in the previous timeline (holding her soul hostage in return for tasks like unblocking the frozen Subcon Well, retrieving something from Queen Vanessa's Manor, etc)
- Cooking Cat approaches Alex, getting to know her and informing her that the Earth Alliance is looking for her, offering help and supplies
- While talking to Alex, CC receives a vision of herself interacting with Hat Kid on the Arctic Cruise - something that hasn't happened yet in this timeline
- It seems that when these characters touch Alex, a glitch with Alex's state of reality causes the characters to see a random memory of hers involving that character
- The next day, Alex explores the underground train station beneath Mafia Town, trying to leave on a train and find a new place on the planet to live
- Mu and HK are hot on the trail and board the same train, riding on the roof
- While on the train, Alex encounters cat people who are unknowingly reciting an episode of internet cartoon Bonus Stage, a glimpse into the nature of the multiverse and how universes imprint on others
- At the Main Station of the Nyakuza Metro, Alex finds the process for taking the metro to her intended destination (the Federal Union of Birds, or 'Bird California' as she calls it) is complicated, and ends up missing a required train, forcing her to find a shelter in an abandoned shelter for the night
- Mu and HK confront her in this shelter and realise she is not a threat. They get her to explain that she had been playing the A Hat in Time game, not realising that her presence was felt in this universe
- At which point the Badge Seller arrives to clarify on the nature of the multiverse, and offers a magical badge to Alex to help her become fully in sync with this universe
- He also explains the differences between their worlds: because all the characters in Hat Kid's world have 'Hit Points', when they are hurt they take damage to their HP, but are otherwise fine with no lasting injuries, like a cartoon - whereas Alex's world is 'realistic', she can sustain wounds and injuries from an attack
- The badge would force her to abide by the rules of the reality she is in when she wears it, meaning she would have HP and be subject to this universes physics, but death would be permanent - not wearing it would have her abide by her reality's rules, but allow her to respawn
- Because she was 'in-between', instead she ended up in The Horizon
- The Horizon is the empty space between universes, usually insulating universes from each other. It is also where parts of dead universes end up. It might also be known as the Void (Undertale) or the Backrooms (internet culture)
- The Horizon is also the place where Badge Seller came into existence, as part of the Horizon is a 'beta' version of Subcon Forest that was scrapped, including the Moonjumper, a malevolent entity whose more docile personality is that of the Badge Seller
- After walking through the Horizon for a while, Alex comes across a de-textured beta version of Subcon Forest, in which Badge Seller is waiting for her with food and companionship
- After a chat, Alex is taken back to the Metro, and Mu and HK take her back to the Spaceship
- HK gathers the Earth Alliance, introduces Alex properly, and Mu finally apologises (helped by HK) for her actions
- After the meeting, HK talks to Snatcher privately and reveals that she had previously found out about the Tale of Queen Vanessa (Snatchers origin story) and apologises for stumbling onto that knowledge, promising that she will keep it to herself
- Snatcher takes Alex and brings her to Subcon Forest to get his own answers about her origin
- During the discussion, Alex drops a "Subcon Deez Nuts" joke and, offended, Snatcher kills her
- Another difference in her world to Snatchers emerges: when people die on this earth, they burst in a puff of smoke and drop Pons and Health Pons (currency and healing, respectively). When people in Alex's world die, their corpse doesn't disappear, and with the wounds Snatcher gives her, a pool of blood forms
- He also receives a vision when he strikes Alex: Snatcher trying to strike a deal with Hat Kid after his defeat by her hand
- Snatcher muses on this, and while coming up with a new plan, sees that Alex has respawned
- Alex apologises for the joke, and Snatcher demands to know about the vision
- After threatening her life, Alex relents and Snatcher psychically invades her mind, interacting with the vision
- Snatcher is the first person to *see* that Alex played this world as a game, and in fact sees that in Alex's version of events, she was in control of Hat Kid (This would be the key to help him realise that Alex also knows about his prior identity as the Prince, but he doesn't think that far yet)
- Seeing what she knows, Snatcher forces Alex into a soul contract like he did with HK in the previous timeline, under threat of constant creative deaths if she refuses - doing work for him like unblocking the frozen Subcon Well and delivering mail for his followers the Subconites (Subcon Minions), the things he would have had HK do
- Somewhere along the way he makes the connection between Alex's knowledge and HK's knowledge of him being the same, and makes good on his promise of torture, putting her through a version of the death he went through to become Snatcher
- This is where DJ Grooves steps in, having been looking for Alex to help her
- Grooves rescues her and Snatcher banishes her from the Forest, keeping her soul
- The detail of Snatcher holding her soul is important, it will allow him to 'feel' where her presence is when the multiverse starts to collapse
- Grooves and Alex travel back to his apartment on the Moon, helping her find residence there
- While on the Moon, Alex becomes working with Badge Seller, interested in his offer to visit other universes
- This is partly influenced by the Moonjumper personality reawakening within Badge Seller's mind, not fully taking over yet
- Moonjumper's ultimate goal is to collapse the entire multiverse (reason unknown as of writing) and become the god of a new one
- DJ Grooves and Conductor attend the Bird Movie Awards Ceremony, where surprisingly, Grooves is declared the winner
- With the new timeline meaning that Hat Kid never lost the Time Pieces, that means both directors never had the hourglasses to show off in their movies, nor could use them as a bargaining chip for Hat Kid to participate
- Which meant that to recruit Hat Kid in the new timeline, DJ Grooves appealed to Hat Kid's childlike wonder for approval and the promise of stardom, helping her obtain a passport so she could work legally for Grooves
- The Conductor, like in the old timeline, made her work for him as well because she was working for Grooves, but without Time Pieces as a bargaining chip and his personality as a self-obsessed grump, HK couldn't summon the same enthusiasm for Conductor's movies as he could for Grooves
- At the end of this arc, Badge Seller (now fully the Moonjumper, but still pretending to be friendly to Alex to accomplish his goal) brings Alex to a core part of the Horizon and has her engage a device, which ultimately destroys the Horizon and pulls its remaining power into Moonjumper
- The result is various universes colliding into each other, causing portal storms where characters from realities can invade or be pulled into other realities
- For Hat Kid and Mu on the spaceship, it means a multiversal collision event where a wormhole portal takes their Spaceship to a version of Earth from the game Undertale, where time rifts and fluctuations are detected
