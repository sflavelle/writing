## Double Life

(Lily struggles to reconcile the memories of Alex with her own. The Guardians step in to help)

A day ago she had arrived on the grassy surface of a new Earth surrounded by monsters and humans.

A day ago she had been ripped apart in the snow after being betrayed.

She was an ~~inkling~~.

She was a ~~human~~.

Through each eye she saw vastly different lives.

She saw Lily, Agent 5 of the New Squidbeak Splatoon, DJing and protecting a new society from the remnants of her past.

She saw Alex, a human who had explored a video game world beyond her screen.

She couldn’t process it.

Two completely different lives, in different bodies, yet at the exact same time.

Eventually her spasming brain, attempting to cope with the contradictory information, the conflicting realities she both lived, pulled her into sleep, to attempt to process it all.

——

Inkopolis Square was empty.

She looked down at herself, raising an arm.

She saw her own ~~hairless, boneless skin~~ as she gazed at herself.

She saw her own ~~hairy, freckly arm, bent at the elbow~~ as she gazed at herself.

Why… was this happening to her? What had she done to deserve this?

“~~Good question!~~”

“~~What an apt question.~~”

They looked up.

Eyes landed on a skeleton monster, physically similar to the one that had welcomed her to their safe living space.

Eyes landed on a skeleton, an animated creature she had only seen online.

Their shared vision disagreed on what it was wearing, though. Was it golden, flashy garb? Was it a slime-coated hoodie and a cyan eye?

They slowly closed their left eye. The gold disappeared, and the inkling body vanished, now seeing an entirely human self.

They closed their right eye next. The slimy skeleton vanished, the smiling skeleton replacing them, and her inkling self came into full view, replacing the human body she had also occupied.

They tried to speak, opening both eyes despite the disorientation. “What… am I?” They asked, in two voices.

“You’re going to be okay,” reassured a warm, friendly voice.

“Let us find out,” said a firm, formal voice.

The skeleton approached them, and with a ~~golden-garbed~~ ~~corruption-coated~~ arm, took hold of your ~~left~~ ~~right~~ forearm.

“Brace yourself,” said both voices.

And then they began to *pull*.

She screamed out, feeling as if she were being torn in half—

Before something came loose and suddenly Lily was yanked away, stumbling to her left, steadied by a golden skeleton. He held her close as she shuddered from the experience, holding her head.

The inkling turned to see the result-

And saw a human female opposite her, staring at her in shock, similarly held by the dark skeleton.

“…I posit a theory, brother,” he said to the counterpart holding Lily. “We have never seen two souls of your type merge into one being like this. But… I suspect that if this *were* to occur, as it has here, then perhaps there is some origin the two of you share.”

“I… what origin could we share?” Said the human. “She’s clearly a squid kid. Their civilisation is post-apocalyptic or something.”

“I wasn’t always though,” Lily responded, warily. “I… used to be human, way back when. But even then,” she glanced at the skeleton, “I was a guy back then, not her. So… I’m not sure on your theory.”

“Nonetheless,” the brighter skeleton continued, “this thing happening seems very important. And I want to help you make sense of your new memories. Since… you *are* both one person now. And if we don’t… reconcile that, help you get them into place, well… I don’t think humanoid brains handle that sort of thing very well.”
