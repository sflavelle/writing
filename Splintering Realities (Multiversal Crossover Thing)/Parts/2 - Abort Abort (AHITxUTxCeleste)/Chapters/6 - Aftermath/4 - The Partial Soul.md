——

In a corner of the destroyed town, Flowey was proceeding to freak the fuck out.

The spirit of Chara had been around, all this time.

They had *outed* him. In front of the whole town.

And then they had ripped out some other human’s soul and stolen their body before vanishing with the masked clown that fought Snowdin and the Royal Guard and *won*.

This was all too much.

His gaze drifted back to the soul that had been carelessly thrown away.

There were tinges of purple left, as well as whatever solid matter was settled at the bottom. But it was nothing he had ever seen before. Never seen a soul so… hollow.

He could see clearly that there was nothing he could do with this soul. Nothing anyone could do - there was too little power left in this to do anything with, much less break a barrier or become a God.

Snowy footsteps in his direction caused him to snap to attention. He turned, about to flee - he was NOT ready to answer questions about his past - but paused when he saw it was the two younger interlopers.

Mu and Hat Kid looked between him and the floating soul he was observing.

He sighed, and levelled them with a flat stare. “What, come to laugh at what I have become?” He looked away. “Please, just… leave me to my misery.”

The girls looked between each other, and silently walked closer, taking seats around the object of the flower’s interest.

“What is that?” Mu asked, nodding towards the object. “I mean, I can see it’s shaped like a heart, but… I doubt that is Alex’s actual heart.”

“It’s… a soul.” Flowey looked slightly confused. “Though I’ve never seen a soul look so… drained.” He blinked. “Is… Alex the name of that human Chara took control of?”

Hat Kid nodded. “Yeah. We’re… trying to rescue her and stop that Moonjumper guy that stole her, but… I guess we failed at that, huh.” Mumbling, she continued, “I didn’t even think you could die without a soul. I mean I didn’t…”

“Of course you do!” Now Flowey was staring at her with an odd look. “Your soul is… waaaait. Souls work differently where you come from, don’t they?”

“I mean, yeah.” Hattie shrugged. “Like sure you feel empty and kinda muted without one, at least when Snatcher took mine, but…”

“…huh.” The flower paused, processing the thought of a ‘soul snatcher’ whose victims continued to live. Then its mouth ticked upward in a light smile. “Then… I guess someone should show you how things work around here! At least *one* thing will follow the script this time!” He glanced between the two. “Who wants to volunteer for a soul demonstration? Hopefully I can get across just how odd this soul in particular is!”

Mu cautiously raised her hand. “If I do this, you’re not gonna… do something with it, are ya?”

Flowey made a show of thinking about it, bobbing on his stem. “I mean I *could*… but what’s the point?” His expression soured. “This timeline’s screwed, I can’t reset, everyone knows who I used to be, and if I was going to kill you, who’s going to make Chara and that other guy suffer for what they’ve done? I guess lil’ ol’ me would have to do!”

With that, Flowey initiated the encounter, and Mu felt a brief tugging in her chest before…

A bright yellow soul appeared before her. She stared at it, awed. “Wow! That’s so… vivid.”

“Isn’t it?” Flowey grinned. “THAT is how your soul appears here. The combination of your being. *Justice*, huh?”

“What do you mean, ‘this timeline is screwed’?” Hat Kid asked, sitting outside of the encounter.

“Well that Moonjumper *fuck* wasn’t meant to be here at all, is he?” Flowey rolled his eyes. “Here, kid, try and move your soul around to avoid this bullet!” He summoned a petal bullet projectile, lazily weaving it toward Mu’s exposed soul.

“Well neither are we, and you didn’t seem too thrilled about that to begin with either.” Mu swung her soul out of the way, and Flowey’s bullet changed direction to follow it.

“Well yeah, if it wasn’t for you lot, maybe the other kid would be slaughtering by now!” His grin became more maniacal, before sagging. “Or maybe they’d be trying to be nice and trying to befriend Undyne or something while she throws spears at ‘em. You know I had to witness them trying to flirt with Moldsmals the first go around?” He shuddered, refocusing by increasing his projectile’s pace.

“The first go around?” Hat Kid raised an eyebrow. “You make it sound like a time loop.”

“It *is*!” Flowey beamed. “It’s great! You get to murder all you like, then go back and pretend like it never happened!”
